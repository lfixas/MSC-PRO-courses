About Me

I discovered Minecraft in 2009 while searching the internet for something like 'a first-person game similar to Penguin Club.' I stumbled upon a gameplay video where two people were building a castle and using an infinite lava source to illuminate the walls of a tunnel (a very precise but accurate memory). I initially played the cracked version of the game and introduced it to around 80% of my schoolmates at the time, considering that Minecraft was not widely known.
After creating several dozen houses in solo mode and losing them during explorations, I realized that the only solution was to start anew. Around 2013, I ventured into multiplayer gameplay. While I had tried various types of servers, I found a particular fondness for semi-roleplay (semi-rp) servers. These servers allowed me to engage in a wide range of activities, from building to redstone contraptions to resource farmingâ€”everything I loved.
Fast forward to today, and I still have a deep affection for this game. I continue to embark on new projects and explore the vast possibilities within Minecraft, always learning more about its intricacies.
